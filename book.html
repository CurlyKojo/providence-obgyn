Her story

Why Providence exists.

For twenty years, Dr. Symone practiced inside bigger systems — nine-minute visits, rotating panels, the quiet frustration of not being able to truly know the women she was caring for. Good medicine, yes, but not the kind she'd been called to.

Providence is the answer to that ache. A solo practice by design: one doctor, one familiar face, and the unhurried time good medicine actually requires. Appointments run thirty to sixty minutes. Every message is read by Dr. Symone. Every story is remembered.

"When a woman walks into my office, I want her to feel two things — that she has been heard, and that she will be remembered. Everything else we do flows from that."

Dr. Symone is a Las Vegas native, trained on the East Coast, and boarded in Obstetrics & Gynecology since 2005. Over two decades she's walked with hundreds of women through their first visits, their first pregnancies, their hardest diagnoses, and the quiet seasons in between. She delivers at Summerlin Hospital and sees patients exclusively at our Summerlin office — one address, one phone number, one doctor.

Outside of medicine, she is a wife, a mother of three, and a person of deep faith. Those parts of her life inform — though never dictate — the care you'll receive. Every patient is welcomed exactly as she is.

The practice

A small set of non-negotiables.

Every decision at Providence — from our room size to our scheduling rhythm — runs through these five convictions.

i.

Time is the medicine.

Thirty to sixty minutes per visit. No nine-minute hallways. The answers we need take the time they take.

ii.

One doctor. Every visit.

You will never be handed off mid-pregnancy. You will never explain your story twice. Continuity is the point.

iii.

Evidence above opinion.

Modern, evidence-based guidelines govern every clinical recommendation — including the ones that are hard to hear.

iv.

Dignity before efficiency.

The exam room is a respected conversation. Warm gowns, private dressing, no one enters without a knock.

v.

Every woman. Every season.

First periods to last postpartum visits, teen exams to menopause consultations — all welcomed, all kept.
