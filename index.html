For new patients.

Welcome. Here's what to expect before you walk in the first time.

Is Providence accepting new patients?

+

Yes — with a deliberately small panel. Because Providence is a solo practice, we only welcome as many patients as Dr. Symone can truly know. We aim to confirm new-patient visits within two weeks of your request.

What happens at a first visit?

+

Every new-patient visit is sixty minutes long and begins seated, fully dressed, in our consult room. We go through your history, your questions, and your hopes for care before any exam is offered. When an exam is appropriate, you're walked to a private exam room with warmed linens.

You'll leave with a written plan and a clear sense of what (if anything) needs following up.

What should I bring?

+

Your insurance card and a photo ID. If you have them: a list of medications, previous gynecologic records, and the completed new-patient intake form (we email it when your visit is confirmed). That's it.

How do I get to the office?

+

We're in the Summerlin Medical Plaza, Suite 200, with free ground-level parking directly in front. Full directions and a map live on our contact page.

Appointments & visits.

How we schedule, message, and keep your time respected.

How long will I wait for an appointment?

+

For established patients: most routine concerns are seen within a week. Annuals are booked a few weeks out. For new patients, we typically confirm within two weeks. Urgent concerns are always triaged same-day.

Will I always see Dr. Symone?

+

Yes. Every clinical visit at Providence is with Dr. Symone herself. No rotating providers, no mid-pregnancy hand-offs. If she's unavailable (rare — typically illness or family emergency), we reschedule rather than substitute.

How do I message the office between visits?

+

Existing patients use our secure patient portal for clinical questions, refill requests, and results. Messages are read by Dr. Symone herself and answered within one business day.

What about after-hours concerns?

+

Established patients have access to an after-hours line answered by Dr. Symone's on-call coverage. For true emergencies — heavy bleeding, severe pain, any concern about your baby — call 911 or proceed directly to Summerlin Hospital's Labor & Delivery or Emergency Department.

What if I need to cancel?

+

Please give us 24 hours when you can. Because our schedule is small by design, a cancelled slot means another patient could have been seen — we appreciate the courtesy, and we extend the same to you.

Pregnancy & delivery.

What obstetric care looks like under one doctor.

Where do you deliver?

+

Dr. Symone delivers at Summerlin Hospital Medical Center. She attends her own patients' deliveries whenever possible, including nights and weekends. Backup coverage is arranged with one trusted OBGYN partner for the rare occasion she's unavailable.

Do you support natural birth? VBAC? Epidurals?

+

All of the above. Our birth philosophy is that you know your body — our job is evidence-based guidance and the safest delivery possible. VBACs are welcomed when medically appropriate. Birth plans are drafted together around week 34.

What about high-risk pregnancies?

+

Many high-risk pregnancies are safely co-managed between Dr. Symone and a maternal-fetal medicine specialist. She'll coordinate referrals if needed, and — importantly — stay as your primary OBGYN throughout.

Do you offer in-office ultrasound?

+

Yes — in every prenatal visit, which means dating scans, heartbeat checks, and reassurance happen with Dr. Symone, not a separate facility. Detailed anatomy scans are performed at a partner imaging center.

Insurance & billing.

Transparent, plain-language, no surprises.

Which plans do you accept?

+

Most major PPO plans: Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna, Anthem, Tricare, Sierra Health Options, and select United Healthcare plans. The full list is on our services page — and if you don't see yours, call us. We'll verify.

What if I'm self-pay?

+

We offer transparent self-pay rates for visits, procedures, and prenatal packages. Ask our front desk for a full price sheet — no surprises, no bundled extras.

Do you take Medicaid?

+

Not at this time. We're working with the state on future network participation — please call for current status.

How do billing questions work?

+

Our billing is handled in-house, not outsourced. For any question, call our front desk directly — a real person, no phone tree, no ticket number.

About the practice.

The quiet questions we hear most often about how Providence runs.

Is Providence a faith-based practice?

+

Dr. Symone is a person of personal faith — you'll feel it in the warmth of our office and the name she chose for the practice. But our care is evidence-based first, always. Every patient is welcomed exactly as she is, and every clinical recommendation follows modern OBGYN guidelines.

What does "Providence" mean?

+

It's a word Dr. Symone loves — being watched over with care. She chose it because it described both how she wanted her patients to feel, and how she'd been cared for by the doctors who shaped her.

Can I meet you before becoming a patient?

+

Absolutely. We offer no-cost 15-minute "meet & greet" calls with Dr. Symone for prospective new patients — especially those deciding on a prenatal practice. Request one on our contact page.

Do you ever close the practice?

+

We briefly pause new-patient intake when our panel gets full, to protect the pace of care we've promised existing patients. When that happens, we keep a gentle waitlist.

Still have a question?

Ask, and we'll answer.

If something's on your mind that isn't covered here, send a note — or call during office hours and someone will pick up.
